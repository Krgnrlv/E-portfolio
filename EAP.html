Reflection on introduction week (16.09.2024)

The introduction week at MIE was a mix of excitement and useful information. We were given a clear idea of what to expect as students and how to navigate our new responsibilities. They talked a lot about how we’re becoming adults now and what that means—basically managing our own time and studies. It felt a bit overwhelming at times, but overall, it was a good way to start feeling like a college student. I think that method that Irish teachers use here is better for students, since it is not only about grades, it’s about our personal opinions and interest. Because of that, I look forward to studying here this year and experience new way of learning on myself!

Folk project №2 (14.10.2024)

Tir na nÓg in six dimensions

  • Individualism vs. Collectivism (IDV):

    • The Tir na nÓg tale leans towards collectivism, as it emphasizes bonds within a community and the importance of family. Oisín’s insistence on going back to his homeland even after all those years away can be tied to his loyalty to his family and his people. This may also show us that Oisín’s culture is one that values the community over individual pursuits.

  • Indulgence vs. Restraint (IVR):

    • The culture portrayed in the tale Tir na nÓg contains elements of indulgence, especially in the descriptions of the faerie realm. In this story, the setting of Tir na nÓg is a paradise-like place where one can find a lot of joy and happiness, indulge in pleasures, and satisfy all needs. In the story, the author briefly mentions that Oisín has spent hundreds of years in Tir na nÓg, conveying that indulgence and pleasure can make time pass by particularly quickly because the brain is already immersed in numb happiness. Excessive indulgence can make people lack the ability to think independently and do things they normally wouldn’t do. When Oisín returns to Ireland, everything familiar to him no longer exists, and three hundred years have disappeared in the blink of an eye in his pleasure.

  • Uncertainty Avoidance Index (UAI):

    • The story of Tir na nÓg shows a culture with low uncertainty avoidance, as Oisín willingly embarks on the journey to a unknown and mysterious realm without fully knowing the consequences of this action. His acceptance of Niamh’s invitation despite the risks it presents, reflects a culture that values exploration and is open to uncertainties in life. Oisín’s adventurous spirit represents a community that embraces these uncertainties as opportunities for growth and experience.

  • Masculinity vs. Femininity (MAS):

    • There are very obvious female characteristics in this story, such as a beautiful white horse and a beautiful princess in the land of youth. However, women are always limited in this story, and the image of the princess is only beautiful. The princess in the land of youth is just a princess, but Oisín appears as a great hero in the story. In comparison, the princess is given less power and energy, and she tried to prevent Oisín from returning to Ireland but was unsuccessful. This represents that she does not have a high voice in the family and has tolerated many concessions.

Language for comparison and contrast (28.10.2024)

Studying at a Local College

Local colleges usually have low tuition fees and living costs which makes quality education affordable. Students can reside with their family which saves them even more money. Local colleges are more convenient as well because students are more likely to get personalized attention . In addition, these colleges are more flexible with their working hours which helps students with employment or family matters. Nevertheless, local colleges may not have features such as a wide variety of courses, opportunities for research and little to no industry connections. They may also have a limited social life and few extracurricular activities as well. Even though local colleges have a relaxing atmosphere and cost-effective tuition, students with the ambition to get specialized programs or industry connections will come across several challenges.

Studying at a University in the Capital City

A university in the capital city offers top-quality education, better facilities, and strong industry connections. Students have access to expert professors, specialized programs, and a diverse academic environment. There are more networking events, internships, and career opportunities, increasing employment prospects. However, living costs, tuition fees, and daily expenses are significantly higher. Students often need to adapt to a fast-paced, competitive atmosphere. While social life is vibrant, with numerous extracurricular activities, large class sizes may mean less individual attention from professors. Despite the challenges, studying in the capital can be an exciting and rewarding experience, preparing students for global career opportunities.

Reflect on peer-review process (04.11.2024)

Participating in the peer-review process using the Ladder of Feedback model was a helpful and new experience for me as an international student. Before the process, I felt a little nervous because I was unsure how my classmates would view my work. I also worried about whether my feedback for others would be useful. However, I was also excited to get comments to improve my draft.

During the process, I felt more comfortable because the Ladder of Feedback had a clear structure. It started with asking questions, then moved to positive comments, and finally gave space for suggestions and concerns. This made the feedback feel respectful and helpful. I enjoyed reading my peer’s work because it helped me think critically about writing. When I received feedback, it was encouraging to hear about my strengths, but the suggestions for improvement were the most valuable.

After the session, I felt more confident about revising my draft. I used the feedback to make unclear parts more detailed and to improve the flow of ideas. Overall, the process was better than I expected. Next time, I will prepare better questions about my work and take more notes during the discussion. This experience helped me both as a writer and a reviewer.

Folk project №3 (21.10.2024)

Comprising of «Tír na nÓg» and «Urashima Taro» tales.

In both the «Tír na nÓg» and «Urashima Taro» tales we can see that there are themes of time, immortality, and the consequences of acting without thinking of the outcome.

One of the similarities of these two tales is a general story concept. Both tales have a main character who spends more time than they intended in another realm, only to find that everything they once knew is now gone. In both stories the main characters are tempted by the promise of a paradise or reward, but in both stories, this comes at a cost. In «Tír na nÓg», returning to his homeland causes Óisín’s death; in «Urashima Taro», opening the box causes Taro to age rapidly and as a result of this: his death.

Another similarity of «Tír na nÓg» and «Urashima Taro» tales is symbolism. The paradise described in «Tír na nÓg» and the Dragon Palace in «Urashima Taro» are both realms that symbolize a place beyond human understanding, where time passes differently, and human desires are fulfilled. Both places highlight the temptation of the unknown and the dangers it may hold. In both tales, the act of returning to home comes with a confrontation of reality and mortality. Both Óisín and Taro had to face the fact that their families were now gone and the land they once lived in was unrecognizable to them.

While «Tír na nÓg» and «Urashima Taro» share common themes of time and consequence, they are shaped by their distinct cultural contexts. In first story Óisín didn’t thought about his family and friends a lot when he decided to go to the magic land. That can show us that Irish society of that time was more individualistic. However, in second story Taro was about to reject invitation to go to the Sea Palace, because of his sick and old mother, but then he still agrees, because he thought it wouldn't be for a long time. It tells us about more collectivistic society in Japan. Also, this story clearly expresses the role of nature in human life, which we couldn’t see in «Tír na nÓg».

Both stories emphasize the passage of time and the nature of mortality. In the magical lands, the heroes live in pleasure, but upon returning to the mortal world, they face the harsh reality of time catching up.

In conclusion, it is always very interesting how, it would seem, completely different in their culture nations have common moral principles and values and even come up with very similar plots. Of course, each story has its own unique features by which we can say about society of each individual folk. Therefore, it is important to get acquainted with tales and learn more about your own and other nations through them.

Review of Folk project (04.11.2024)

While I was doing our folk project, I realized that folktales are a fascinating way to learn about different cultures. It was even more interesting for someone like me who is now experiencing traditions from the outside. I noticed that folktales bring culture to life in a way that feels natural and easy to understand, even without a detailed explanation. Through the characters, conflicts, and values presented in these stories, I understood what each society values and how people from that culture think about things like family, respect, or courage. As an example, in the Irish tale of Tír na nÓg, Oisín travels to the mystical land of youth, this story emphasizes values like bravery, love, and the charm of adventure.

One of the insights I learned from this project is how similar some folktales are, even from cultures on opposite sides of the world. Themes like the journey of life, love, or explanation of the origin of geographical objects are present in many folktales, showing the connection we all share through similar life experiences. This universality of certain ideas and values surprised me because I used to think of each culture as very distinct, but now, I feel like these shared stories can help bring people closer together, creating understanding across cultural divides.

As for the Hofstede culture matrix, it is helpful as a general tool but somewhat limiting. It breaks down cultural values into categories like individualism vs. collectivism and power distance, which helped me understand certain patterns, but it doesn’t capture the full complexity that I noticed in the folktales. Folktales often reveal a lot of mixed values and contradictions within a culture, showing that culture is not always consistent or straightforward.

Personally, this project has changed the way I see cultural differences. I feel more respectful and curious about the unique values behind certain traditions and beliefs, also this experience will make me more open-minded when meeting people from other cultures in the future, recognizing that everyone background is shaped by these deep-rooted stories. It’s also encouraged me to look for similarities with others rather than only focusing on differences, seeing how our shared narratives can bring us together.

Reflection on annual examination (27.01.2025)

Reviewing my exam prep and performance allows me to understand how I can further improve. As I have looked over my study schedule, I came across a realistic restriction that I had developed. Certain subjects were left with significantly more focus than others, and some topics within subjects were highly glossed over. For the future, I will focus on more effective time management so that I can cover each topic sufficiently. Retrospectively predicting exam topics proved to have mixed results - I was accurate in some areas, yet missed a fair amount of concepts. I will aim to broaden my scope for revision in the future.

On the exam day, I made sure to skim the paper before attempting it. This gave me a rough idea of the questions and how many marks each question would provide me. In this instance, I failed to devise a plan as to which questions needed to be answered first. Although I did identify the easier questions, I found myself spending an inappropriate amount of time on those, causing me to rush when answering the higher value and more challenging questions. My time management strategy needs adaptation. I will make an effort to remember allocating time based participants’ needs, focusing mainly on the more scoring questions first while ensuring that time will be available to attempt all questions.

I felt a combination of anxiety and confidence at the same time. Questions that needed memorization were fairly easy, however essay questions were extremely difficult. During uncertain times, I would not trust myself which led to further procrastination. Practicing more under these conditions would enable me to instill a sense of confidence and effectiveness for time constrained situations.

For my future exams, I will attempt to balance all topics within all my study sessions, use more practice tests in order to anticipate the types of questions that can be posed, learn how to manage my time appropriately, and focus on remaining calm during the test. This insight revealed the need to be strict with revision schedules, develop an improved questioning strategy, along with reinforced self-belief to be helpful in future interactions.
